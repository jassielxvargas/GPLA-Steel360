Operating Concept

We would receive the project contract drawings showing end reactions, along with a “rough” structural model, essentially LOD 300 with columns and beams only and no connections. The Dominican Republic team would perform the labor-intensive portion of the connection modeling and preliminary design work. Then, depending on workload, one or two senior engineers in the United States would review, finalize, and stamp the calculations as required.

Key Advantages

  • Connections are added directly into the model, reducing back-and-forth clarification and RFIs.
  • Turnaround time improves because modeling and engineering progress in parallel rather than sequentially.
  • Licensed engineers can focus on review, coordination, and decision-making instead of production tasks.
  • We gain meaningful cost efficiency while maintaining direct control over quality, standards, and priorities.
  • The team develops long-term familiarity with our preferences and becomes an extension of our organization.

For context, current labor rates for this team range between $16 and $19 per hour, not including software licensing such as SDS/2 and RAM Connection, which my current employer already provides. I would be interested to understand how this compares to the rates for the existing team in India.

Initially, we would rely on commercial software to design the connections. However, within a few months, I believe we can begin transitioning toward internally developed tools that will improve consistency, transparency, and confidence in the results. I have the technical capability to support the development of this software.

Steel360 for Delivery of Project Information to Fabricators and/or Erectors

As I have mentioned, I have been working on a web app, named Steel360, which allows us to deliver project information to our clients in a simple, organized way. Through a secure portal, clients can access:

  • Contract drawings
  • 3D model
  • 2D shop and erection drawings

Our clients will be able to navigate and explore the drawings and model in a straightforward way. This helps clients understand the work visually, reduces confusion, and supports better coordination conversations.

Steel360 also centralizes documents, maintains version control, and keeps everything in one place instead of being spread across emails and shared drives. Project information is portable and can be accessed from anywhere via a computer or iPad, since all content is stored in the cloud.

This capability is an added-value project tool that we can include when appropriate and when it aligns with the scope and commercial terms of the project, not as a generic free service, but as part of a coordinated delivery approach.
